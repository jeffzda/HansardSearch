Here's the Hansard policy insight extraction pipeline we discussed:
Stage 1: Single extraction pass
Extract rich contextual metadata from each parliamentary contribution:

Bill name (if explicitly mentioned)
Speaker role, party, chamber, date
Key actors and organizations mentioned
Subject keywords and policy domain signals
Apparent purpose (advocating, questioning, reporting, recommending)
A "what_discussed" summary broader than just delivery insights
Any explicit continuity/discontinuity language ("this bill reintroduces..." or "we are abandoning the previous approach...")

Stage 2: Policy initiative clustering

Primary clustering: Timestamp + topic for contributions close in time with topical overlap
Orphan reconciliation: Maximum confidence topic matching for contributions that don't cluster naturally
Cross-parliament handling: Strong topic matches across electoral boundaries get moderate confidence; weak matches get heavy penalty

Stage 3: Timeline assembly
Build policy initiative records from clustered contributions showing:

First parliamentary appearance
Committee referrals and evidence sessions
Key arguments and counter-arguments presented
Amendment attempts and outcomes
Passage, failure, or abandonment
Implementation signals and subsequent reviews

Stage 4: Entity resolution for long-delayed policies
The hardest part - recognizing when contributions separated by years are actually the same policy story. Uses explicit language detection for policy continuity/discontinuity and confidence scoring for potential matches.
The output would be policy initiative timelines as the unit of analysis - complete decision arcs rather than individual contributions.
For the taxonomy design, we'd need to define the policy lifecycle phases, failure modes, and outcome classes equivalent to what you built for ARENA but adapted to parliamentary processes.
